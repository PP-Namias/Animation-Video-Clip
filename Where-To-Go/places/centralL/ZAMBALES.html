About Zambales

Destination

Welcome to Zambales

Want to quickly get away to a beach and island from Metro Manila's busy cities? Drive or board a bus to go to Zambales! Because of its location on the western shores of Luzon, near the West Philippine Sea, Zambales is blessed with natural attractions for sun seekers. It has numerous coves and islands, including, to name a few, Anawangin Cove, Nagsasa Cove, Potipot Island, and Capones Island. Subic is a popular tourist destination in the North, and it is also located in Zambales. The summit and crater lake of Mount Pinatubo, an active stratovolcano, are likewise located in the Botolan region of Zambales.

See And Do

Spend a night or two in the Anawangin Cove or Nagsasa Cove by packing your camping supplies. These coves in Zambales are special because they contain agoho trees, or pine trees, which developed after the eruption of Mt. Pinatubo in 1991. You can go island hopping while camping in these coves and visit surrounding islands including Potipot Island and Capones Island. Exploring Mt. Pinatubo on foot or in a 4x4 is another thing you must do while visiting Zambales. Zambales is a surfing hotspot as well; at Crystal Beach Resort, you may catch waves. Additionally, you can tour Subic, a former US Naval Base. Try exciting outdoor pursuits at Tree Top Adventure, interact with animals at Zoobic Safari or Ocean Adventure, or have fun on a gigantic unicorn-themed inflatable playground.

Where To Go

Zambales is a popular weekend getaway location for city inhabitants, thus there are a variety of lodging options available there, including hotels, resorts, and private homes for rent for all price ranges.
