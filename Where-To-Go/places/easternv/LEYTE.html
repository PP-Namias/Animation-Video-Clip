Welcome to Leyte

The lovely and hospitable province of Leyte is one of the many tourist sites in Visayas. The longest bridge in the nation, the picturesque Kalanggaman Island with its white powdery sand beach, and a significant World War II location can all be found in this province. In addition to this, Leyte is a top location for scuba diving, waterfalls, and additional gorgeous beaches.

When it comes to tourism attractions, Leyte is diverse. Leyte is a must-see when in the Philippines because it has both historical sites and beautiful islands for the ideal vacation. The Leyte Landing Memorial Park, where General Douglas MacArthur landed and uttered the famous phrase "I shall return," must be visited as part of any tour to Leyte. The gorgeous Kalanggaman Island, which is located in Leyte, is known for its pristine waters and fine, white sand. Leyte is also home to the San Juanico Bridge, which connects Leyte to Samar and is the longest bridge in the nation. Beautiful natural beauties can be found in Leyte, such as the guitar-shaped lake in Lake Danao National Park, where you may enjoy swimming, kayaking, or fishing.

The cities of Tacloban and Ormoc, as well as resorts close to the beach areas, have a wide variety of hotels, inns, and transitory houses that are ideal for any budget.
