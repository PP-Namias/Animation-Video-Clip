Welcome to Northern Samar

Northern Samar spans across several square kilometers, stretching from the Pacific Ocean in the East to the Samar Sea in the West. Within this province lie the captivating "Balicuatro Islands" of Biri, Capul, San Antonio, and San Vicente, each offering a blend of adventure and natural beauty with their unique attractions.

Biri is renowned for its seven awe-inspiring geologic rock formations, reminiscent of Australia's 12 Apostles. These formations stand as a breathtaking spectacle for visitors. Capul, on the other hand, holds a rich historical significance as it served as an anchorage for the Manila-Acapulco Galleon Trade during the 17th century. The town still preserves a relatively intact Fortress Church, and the northernmost tip features the Faro, a lighthouse that stands as a symbol of the Golden East. Additionally, visitors can learn Capul's unique dialect known as "Abaknon," derived from the Austronesian language of the Sama-Badjao.

San Vicente takes pride in its Pink Sand Beach found in Sila Island, offering a picturesque and distinctive coastal experience. Meanwhile, San Antonio is the most developed among the four island-municipalities, boasting a chain of resorts along its eastern shores. Catarman, the capital of Northern Samar, is home to the University of Eastern Philippines, which serves as a repository of the region's rich history and cultural heritage.

In Palapag, remnants of a town steeped in historical significance tied to the Sumuroy Rebellion can be explored. The church ruins and centuries-old watchtower are captivating sights to behold. Notably, in 1999, the internationally renowned ELF Authentic Adventure started its operations in the hinterlands of Northern Samar, attracting adventurers with its diverse and exciting challenges.
