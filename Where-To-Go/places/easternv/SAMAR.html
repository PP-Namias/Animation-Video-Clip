Welcome to Samar

Samar province in the Philippines offers a unique beauty that lies in its embrace of nature's gifts rather than flashy accommodations or man-made attractions. It beckons visitors to go back to basics and appreciate the natural wonders scattered throughout the province. Explore Asia's second-largest cave system, the Langon-Gobingob Caves in Calbiga, or venture into the world-renowned Sohoton Caves and Natural Bridge. Experience the thrill of the Ulot River Torpedo Boat Ride in Paranas and savor the delicious flavors of Tenani cuisine.

Discover the majestic rock formations in Marabut, where you can even kayak around them. Unwind and enjoy the soothing sounds of cicadas at the Caluwayan Palm Island Resort. Learn about mangroves by trekking through the 750-hectare Mangrove Forest Safari in Pinabacdao, Samar's agricultural capital. Alternatively, visit San Juan by the Bay and dine at floating cottages while enjoying a view of the iconic San Juanico Bridge.

Samar is also a haven for food enthusiasts. Sample the famous tamales and a variety of Pili-based delicacies in Catbalogan City. Embark on a food tour in Calbayog City, where you can indulge in the succulent Tinapa (smoked fish) and Alimango (Mud Crabs). As an added attraction, catch a special command performance by the Calbayog City Youth Orchestra.

In Samar, the true allure lies in its authentic experiences and the bounties of nature, making it a destination worth exploring for those seeking a genuine connection with the natural world and rich local culture.
