Welcome to catanduanes

Catanduanes is an island province located in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. It is situated in the eastern part of Luzon, the country's largest island. Known as the "Land of the Howling Winds" due to its exposure to typhoons, Catanduanes offers a blend of natural wonders, rich history, and a laid-back island lifestyle.

Geographically, Catanduanes is located in the easternmost part of the Bicol Peninsula. It is bordered by the Philippine Sea on the north and east, and the Maqueda Channel on the south. The island is approximately 64 kilometers long and 32 kilometers wide, with a rugged coastline, rolling hills, and mountains.

Catanduanes is known for its breathtaking landscapes and pristine beaches. One of its popular attractions is Puraran Beach, a renowned surfing spot with its famous "Majestic" wave. Other notable beaches include Mamangal Beach, Twin Rock Beach, and Balite Beach, where visitors can enjoy swimming, sunbathing, and beach activities.

The province is blessed with lush greenery and natural wonders. Binurong Point is a picturesque cliff overlooking the vast ocean, offering panoramic views and a challenging trek. Maribina Falls, Gigmoto Hidden Paradise, and Luyang Cave are also worth exploring for their beauty and unique experiences.

Inland, Catanduanes boasts a rich and diverse ecosystem. It is home to dense forests, cascading waterfalls, and abundant flora and fauna. The Catanduanes Watershed Forest Reserve is a protected area that serves as a habitat for various endemic species and is an ideal destination for nature enthusiasts and eco-tourism.

Catanduanes has a colorful history influenced by different cultures. The province has been inhabited by indigenous tribes, and traces of their heritage can still be seen today. The Catanduanes Museum in Virac showcases artifacts and exhibits that highlight the province's cultural and historical significance.

Festivals play an important role in Catanduanes' cultural identity. The Abaca Festival, held annually in May, celebrates the province's primary agricultural product, abaca (Manila hemp). It features street dancing, trade fairs, and various activities showcasing the local culture and industry.

The people of Catanduanes, called Catandunganons, are known for their warm hospitality and resilience in the face of natural disasters. The local cuisine features fresh seafood, coconut-based dishes, and delicacies such as binutong (sticky rice cooked in coconut milk and wrapped in banana leaves) and pili nuts.

In terms of livelihood, agriculture and fishing are the primary industries in Catanduanes. Abaca is the main agricultural crop, contributing to the province's economy. Fishing is also a significant source of livelihood for many communities along the coast.
