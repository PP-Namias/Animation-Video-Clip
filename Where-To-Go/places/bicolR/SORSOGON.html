Welcome to Sorsogon

Sorsogon is a province located in the Bicol Region of the Philippines. It is situated in the southeastern part of Luzon, the country's largest island. Sorsogon is known for its scenic landscapes, natural attractions, and rich cultural heritage.

Geographically, Sorsogon is bordered by the provinces of Albay to the north, Masbate to the east, and Samar to the southeast. It faces the Philippine Sea on the east and the Ticao Pass on the west, which separates it from the island of Ticao.

The province of Sorsogon offers a diverse range of attractions. One of its prominent landmarks is the majestic Mount Bulusan, an active volcano and the highest peak in the province. It is part of the Bulusan Volcano Natural Park, which is a protected area known for its lush forests, hot springs, and beautiful crater lake.

Sorsogon is also blessed with stunning beaches and islands. Subic Beach in Matnog is famous for its pinkish sand and crystal-clear waters. Other notable coastal destinations include Paguriran Island, Donsol Beach, and Gubat Beach. These places offer opportunities for swimming, snorkeling, diving, and island hopping.

Moreover, Sorsogon is renowned as the "Whale Shark Capital of the Philippines." Donsol, a municipality in Sorsogon, is known for its whale shark (locally known as "butanding") interaction tours. From November to June, visitors can witness these gentle giants in their natural habitat and even swim alongside them.

Culturally, Sorsogon is a vibrant province with a rich heritage. It is home to various festivals and events, including the Kasanggayahan Festival, celebrated annually in October to showcase the province's culture and traditions. The Pantomina sa Tinampo, a traditional dance, is often performed during festivities.

The people of Sorsogon, called Sorsoganons, are known for their warm hospitality and strong sense of community. The local cuisine features Bicolano flavors, known for its generous use of chili peppers and coconut milk. Spicy dishes like Bicol Express and Laing are among the popular regional specialties.

In terms of economic activities, agriculture and fishing play vital roles in Sorsogon's economy. The province is known for producing crops such as coconut, abaca (Manila hemp), and rice. Fishing is also a significant industry, with its coastal waters abundant in marine resources.

Overall, Sorsogon is a province that offers a mix of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural experiences. It attracts both local and international tourists looking to explore its stunning landscapes, encounter marine wildlife, and immerse themselves in the rich Bicolano culture.
