Welcome to Siargao Island

Siargao Island, once a hidden gem known only among surfers, has now gained global recognition as one of the most sought-after destinations in the world. Its breathtaking beauty, world-class waves, pristine white sand islets, enchanting turquoise tide pools, and hidden lagoons have earned it the title of the world's most beautiful island in 2018, as proclaimed by Conde Nast Traveler.

Despite its small land area of 437 square kilometers, Siargao offers a diverse range of natural wonders that can be easily explored within a day. From world-class surf spots to picturesque white sand islets, captivating turquoise tide pools, and serene palm tree-lined lagoons, these attractions are located in close proximity to each other. Moreover, the island is now home to newly established resorts and restaurants that cater to both local and international visitors, offering a fusion of local and global cuisines.

Siargao's journey as a surfing paradise began in the 1980s when it gained popularity among surfers seeking an alternative to Bali, Indonesia. The discovery of the barreling right-hander known as Cloud 9, which was later featured in a surf magazine in 1992, attracted global attention and put Siargao on the map as a premier surfing destination.

In recent years, Siargao has experienced a surge in tourism, drawing not only surf enthusiasts but also travelers who appreciate the island's natural beauty beyond the waves. Siargao's allure extends to its pristine beaches, hidden coves, and captivating attractions that lie beyond the bustling towns of General Luna and Catangan.

One of these hidden gems is Sohoton Cove, a 60-hectare cove located in Bucas Grande Island. Accessible by a one-hour boat ride from Dapa port, Sohoton Cove features towering limestone hills adorned with lush tropical vegetation, framing a shoreline of white sand and tiny shells.

Explorers can also venture into the enchanting Hagukan Cave, where the sound of waves against its walls resembles a human snore. To enter the cave, one must swim through a narrow passageway that leads to luminous sapphire-colored water. Nearby, the Magkukuob Cave showcases shimmering stalactites and stalagmites. Exiting the cave requires climbing up a cliff and taking a leap!

For more adventurous spelunkers, Balitas Cave and Crystal Cave offer challenging paths that lead to stunning crystal formations and cool, clear water. The caves are filled with the echoing calls of balinsasayaw birds, also known as swiftlets.

Island hopping is another popular activity in Siargao, as the neighboring islands offer their own paradises. The triple island tour, which includes Guyam Island, Daku Island, and Naked Island, is a must-try experience. Guyam Island, the smallest of the three, can be circumnavigated in just a few minutes, while Daku Island provides ample space for relaxation and a delectable seafood and tropical fruit feast. As for Naked Island, it stands out for its peculiar beauty, consisting solely of pristine white sand without any vegetation or structures. Visitors often have fun taking unique photos, paying tribute to the island's fame by posing naked.

Siargao Island truly captures the hearts of adventure seekers and nature lovers alike, offering a remarkable blend of world-class surfing, stunning landscapes, and hidden gems waiting to be explored.
