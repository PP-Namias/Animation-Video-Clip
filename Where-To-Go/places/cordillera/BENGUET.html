About Benguet

Welcome to Benguet

The Philippines is renowned not only for its stunning islands and beaches but also for its breathtaking natural landscapes, many of which can be found in the province of Benguet. Located at the southernmost part of the Cordillera Administrative Region in northern Luzon, Benguet is often referred to as the Salad Bowl of the Philippines due to its status as a major supplier of fresh vegetables in the country. With its picturesque mountain views, serene ambiance, and refreshing cool breeze, Benguet offers a rejuvenating escape from the bustling city life.

See And Do

Benguet is most famous for being home to Baguio City, widely recognized as the Summer Capital of the Philippines and a top tourist destination. Visitors can explore popular Baguio tourist spots such as Camp John Hay, Burnham Park, and The Mansion, among many others. The capital of Benguet, La Trinidad, is known for its vegetable and strawberry production. Tourists can enjoy the experience of picking their own fresh strawberries and vegetables at local farms for a reasonable price. La Trinidad's abundance of strawberries has led to the creation of delicious strawberry-flavored treats that are a must-try, including strawberry taho (hot silken tofu with sweet syrup and sago pearls), strawberry ice cream, and strawberry jam. Don't miss the vibrant hillside communities of Stonehill, Botiwtiw, and Sadjap (STOBOSA) in La Trinidad, with their colorful houses reminiscent of the favela murals in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. For nature enthusiasts, a hike to Mount Pulag offers the opportunity to witness the captivating sea of clouds. The rewarding view that awaits at the summit makes every step taken well worth the effort.

Where To Go

Accommodation options in Benguet range from budget-friendly to luxurious, with a concentration of hotels and inns primarily in Baguio City, given its popularity among tourists.
