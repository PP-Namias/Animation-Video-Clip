Welcome to Ifugao

When you visit Ifugao, don't miss the opportunity to savor their renowned Tinawon rice, known for its distinct flavor and quality. While on your search for rice, keep an eye out for the bulul, a carved wooden figure that serves as a guardian of their precious rice crops. These figures hold significant cultural and spiritual value to the Ifugao people.

If you're still eager to explore more about rice, head to the UNESCO World Heritage Site known as the Rice Terraces of the Philippines. Marvel at the awe-inspiring Banaue Rice Terraces, which have been ingeniously carved into the mountains by the Ifugao people over 2,000 years ago. Scaling these ancient terraces offers a breathtaking experience and a deeper appreciation for the remarkable engineering skills and agricultural practices of the Ifugaos.

To complete your Ifugao adventure, make sure to attend the Gotad Ad Ifugao festival, held annually in June. This festival showcases the age-old cultures and traditions of the Ifugao people. Immerse yourself in the festivities and participate in activities such as the traditional wooden scooter race, which adds an element of excitement to the celebration.

Visiting Ifugao provides a unique opportunity to delve into the rich agricultural heritage, cultural practices, and festive traditions of this fascinating region.
