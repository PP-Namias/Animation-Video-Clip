Welcome to Cebu

Cebu, apart from its rich history, luxurious resorts, and vibrant nightlife, offers a range of eco-adventures for nature enthusiasts. Within the forests of inland Cebu, hidden waterfalls and rare bird species await those who are willing to explore. Surrounding the 200-kilometer-long island, coral reefs can be found almost everywhere, providing opportunities for boating and scuba diving adventures.

Cebu is a bustling hub with a dynamic and entrepreneurial population. It's common to see locals managing multiple businesses during the week and engaging in recreational activities like boating and diving on weekends. The island's energy and vitality are evident, particularly with its development, such as the South Road Properties (SRP), a reclaimed land area spanning 300 hectares, showcasing Cebu's growth.

Moreover, Cebu is at the forefront of sustainable tourism in the Philippines. The Mactan-Cebu International Airport stands as the country's first solar-powered airport and has implemented a ban on single-use plastics among its concessionaires. Visitors can engage in mindful tourism experiences, such as award-winning mangrove river tours and guided visits to ancestral homes, led by local guides. Cebu's culinary scene is equally impressive, offering a delightful surprise for food enthusiasts, showcasing the region's rich culinary heritage.
