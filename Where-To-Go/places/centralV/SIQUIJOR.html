Welcome to Siquijor

Siquijor, also known as the Mystic Island of Central Visayas, is located in the southernmost part of the Visayan Island Group. It is situated approximately 30 kilometers southeast of Negros Island and about 50 kilometers from the nearest point of northwestern Mindanao. The island consists of six municipalities: Enrique Villanueva, Larena, Lazi, Maria, San Juan, and Siquijor.

During the Spanish colonial period, Siquijor was referred to as Isla de Fuegos or Island of Fire. This name was given because the Spanish explorers observed that the island was illuminated with twinkling lights during the nighttime. In reality, these lights were caused by fireflies inhabiting the molave trees, which were believed to be their home.

Siquijor is a tranquil destination, perfect for those seeking an escape from the fast-paced city life. It offers a haven for hikers, bikers, and nature enthusiasts. The island is surrounded by white sandy beaches stretching across its 102-kilometer shoreline. Additionally, mangroves and marine sanctuaries are scattered around the island, providing a safe environment for its diverse marine life.

One of the notable aspects of Siquijor is its reputation for unique healing arts. The island has a centuries-old tradition of creating local potions, which are still practiced and used as alternative medicine today. Traditional healers gather on the island annually to renew their connection with nature and create fresh batches of these local remedies.
