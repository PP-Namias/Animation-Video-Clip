Welcome to Iloilo

Because of its geographical placement in the middle of the country, Iloilo has been hailed as The Heart of the Philippines. Iloilo is home to historical landmarks, quaint beaches and islands, heritage homes, plus tasty and unique cuisine. The province's picturesque natural wonders and the friendly locals will make this a destination that will feel like a second home.

If you’re a history buff, you won’t get enough of the places that Iloilo has to offer. It's home to different historical churches and religious artifacts. Some of the most popular ones are the Jaro Cathedral, Molo Church, and Miago Church. As with any trip, don’t forget the food. Try the famous La Paz Batchoy and a wide array of seafood delicacies like oysters and scallops waiting for you at some of the most popular restaurants in the province.

As one of the oldest cities in the Philippines, you can also visit heritage houses and mansions built during the Spanish-era in the country. Go inside Molo Mansion, Nelly Gardens, Casa Mariquit, and Camiña Balay nga Bato to name a few.

A must-visit is Islas de Gigantes in Carles. This island-hopping destination is one of the most popular tourist spots in Iloilo, boasting hidden lagoons, stunning islands and beaches, and delectable but affordable seafood buffets that can satisfy any hungry stomach. If you happen to book your trip here in January, don’t miss the Dinagyang Festival. This festival is celebrated on the fourth Sunday of January and is a colorful time in Iloilo. Witness the beauty of Iloilo by booking a trip and discovering the different sites yourself!
