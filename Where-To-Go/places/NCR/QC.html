Welcome to Baguio City

Under the leadership of former President Manuel L. Quezon, Quezon City was envisioned as the new capital of the Philippines and served as the capital from 1948 to 1976. Today, it is the largest city in terms of population in Metro Manila and the second biggest city in the Philippines.

Quezon City has embraced its role as a cultural, historical, and educational hub, making tourism a significant focus. The city aims to provide not only enriching experiences for visitors but also livelihood opportunities within the tourism sector.

Quezon City offers a wide range of tourism offerings that cater to the diverse needs and interests of visitors. Its cultural attractions include museums, art galleries, and historical landmarks that showcase the city's rich heritage and contributions to Philippine history. Educational activities are also prominent, with numerous universities and learning institutions providing opportunities for academic exploration.

In addition to its cultural and educational offerings, Quezon City has been proactive in developing its tourism sector to provide livelihood opportunities for its residents. This competitive direction ensures that the city's tourism industry contributes to economic growth and provides sustainable income sources for local communities.

Overall, Quezon City's tourism thrust combines its cultural, historical, and educational assets with a focus on providing livelihood opportunities. With its diverse tourism offerings, the city is well-equipped to meet the needs of visitors and create a vibrant and thriving tourism sector.
